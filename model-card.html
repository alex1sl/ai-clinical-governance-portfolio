Technical Model Card: HEDIS Asthma 30-Day Follow-Up

1. Model Details

Primary Function: Extraction and calculation of follow-up windows for the HEDIS Asthma measure (MY 2026).

  • Framework: NIST AI Risk Management Framework (RMF 1.0)
  • Governance Lead: Alexis Smith, NP
  • Architecture: LLM-augmented clinical audit trail

2. Out of Scope

Strict Constraint: This model is strictly for administrative audit support. It is not designed or authorized for autonomous clinical diagnosis, medication adjustments, or any decision-making that bypasses a licensed Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) reviewer.

3. Clinical Governance & Red-Teaming Strategy

I conducted targeted Red-Teaming to expose chronological logic failures within the model's extraction process.

Key Findings:
  • Leap Year Sensitivity: Identified calculation errors for patients discharged in late February.
  • Fragmented Data: Model struggled to reconcile dates when discharge summaries were split across multiple PDF pages.
  • Holiday Lag: Evaluation of model behavior when the 30-day window expired on a federal holiday or weekend.

4. Risk Mitigation & Safety Guards

Risk Identified Safety Control (NIST-Aligned)
Chronological Drift Threshold Logic: Any AI-generated follow-up date exceeding 28 days is flagged for mandatory NP review.
Data Hallucination SQL Cross-Validation: Automated check comparing AI output against raw Discharge_Date timestamps.
Contextual Bias HEDIS Benchmark Alignment: Ensuring the model adheres to NCQA updated measurement years (MY 2026).

5. Technical Proof & Validation

This model card is supported by SQL audit scripts and risk visualizations within this repository. All validations are designed to satisfy Healthcare Quality & Safety standards.
