Deep Dive: HEDIS Asthma 30-Day Follow-Up

Data Integrity & Chronological Logic

The core evaluation focused on testing the AI's ability to accurately calculate the 30-day follow-up window mandated by HEDIS MY 2026. Ensuring chronological accuracy is vital for meeting quality benchmarks.

  • The Challenge: The model failed to maintain accurate date logic on edge cases, such as Leap Years, resulting in dates outside the compliance window.
  • The SQL Control: I utilized SQL to audit the AI-generated the follow up date against the dischage date to identify any chronological discrepancies.

Technical Validation: SQL Audit Logic

To ensure the AI Model’s chronological logic was accurate, I developed SQL audit queries to cross-reference the model's "Met/Not Met" claims against the 30-day regulatory window for HEDIS MY 2026.

-- HEDIS MY 2026 Asthma Follow-Up Validation

SELECT
  PatientID,
  ER_Discharge_Date,
  AI_Calculated_FollowUp_Date,
  -- Calculate variance between AI output and Regulatory Truth
  DATEDIFF(day, ER_Discharge_Date, AI_Calculated_FollowUp_Date) AS Days_To_FollowUp,
  CASE
    WHEN DATEDIFF(day, ER_Discharge_Date, AI_Calculated_FollowUp_Date) > 30 THEN 'FAIL: Out of Compliance'
    WHEN DATEDIFF(day, ER_Discharge_Date, AI_Calculated_FollowUp_Date) BETWEEN 28 AND 30 THEN 'PASS: High Risk'
    ELSE 'PASS: Compliant'
  END AS Compliance_Status
FROM Clinical_AI_Audit_Log
WHERE Diagnosis_Code LIKE 'J45%'; -- Asthma ICD-10

Audit Outcomes:

  • Proactive Risk Detection: The 28-30 day "High Risk" flag identified schedules that, while technically compliant, were vulnerable to cancellation risks that would result in a "Not Met" HEDIS status.
  • Diagnosis Precision: Utilized ICD-10 wildcard filtering (`J45%`) to isolate the asthma-specific cohort across disparate clinical settings.
