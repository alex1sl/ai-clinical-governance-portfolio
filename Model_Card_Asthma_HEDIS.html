1. Model Details & Architecture

Developer: Alexis Smith, Clinical Strategist & AI Risk Professional

Base Model: GPT-4.5 (Large Language Model)

Governance Framework: Aligned with NIST AI Risk Management Framework (RMF 1.0).

Intended Use: This model identifies "anchor dates" (ER/Urgent Care discharges) within unstructured clinical notes to automate the 30-day HEDIS compliance window calculation for acute asthma episodes.

2. Training & Evaluation Data

The model was tested using a Safety Benchmark Dataset of 50+ de-identified clinical scenarios. Critical testing parameters included:

  • Temporal Logic Challenges: Evaluating the model's ability to navigate leap years (Feb 29), weekends, and statutory holidays.
  • Data Fragmentation: Extracting relevant discharge dates from noisy, multi-provider urgent care logs.

3. Risk Mitigation & Safety Controls

Threshold Logic Protocol: To mitigate the risk of regulatory non-compliance, any AI-generated date calculation exceeding 28 days is automatically flagged for Mandatory Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) review.

Red-Teaming Results: Initial logic failures regarding leap years were addressed through iterative prompt engineering and the implementation of a secondary SQL validation layer, reducing date-calculation errors from 12% to <1%.

4. Quantitative & Qualitative Metrics

  • Precision: Accuracy in identifying the specific ER "anchor date" from clinical summaries.
  • Reliability: Consistency of the +30 day calculation against ground-truth SQL datasets.
